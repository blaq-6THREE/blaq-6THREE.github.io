Commenting

30 minutes into looking at some 2 year old code written by these hands, it hit me! I did not understand my own code. The variable names looked weird, the methods and their parmeters looked weird, and worse of all, what was supposed to serve as my saving grace (comments) was not present. And when it was, it appeared badly written.

Funny thing is, the source code was for a group project and not one of the group members complained. We were all present in one room whenever we worked on the project yet nt a single soul felt the need to comment through it.

BUT this is bad practice. The present me now suffers the decisions the old me didn’t make which is taking the time to comment though the source code.

This tutorial serves to anwser the questions

  • What are comments?
  • How to comment.
  • How not to comment.

What then are comment?

Commenting as an art is the use of high level language, English (whichever language you speak), to describe what your program does.

As students, we are expected to comment through our source code. I know, Robert. Dr. Great Professor wants us to write comments on what the code is doing, yet we aren't taught to comment let alone comment properly.

We are given the notion that so far as there is a comment, it serves as an explanation, and you’re all set.

But that is not the case. As stated by Dr. Bjarne Stroustrup, creator of C++, "comments should be to say things that you can not do well in code." Meaning, only put comments inside of your code if your code will be difficult to understand when glanced at.

Case 1 will be difficult to understand without comments and Case 2 will have no need for comments as it does a great job of naming variables.

Naturally, most programming languages have 2 commenting approaches to them. They include Inline Commenting & Block Commenting.

Inline Commenting:

Most programming languages have inline comments, but its use is pretty tricky. They serve the purpose of having to explain code that is not self documenting. That is, if it doesn’t make sense at a glance, comment it.

Do not overwhelm yourself or a reader of your code by providing comments for every line of code.

Conclusion:

In conclusion one must diligently put in effort in the selection of the identifiers. This means the names of the variables, classes, methods and what not must serve the purpose of their name.

The importance of this action is that a properly named identifier cuts the reduces the risk of over commenting which in turn increases the chance of ones understanding.

Remember, comments are not for your present self, but for your future self, potential inheritors of your program, and anyone.

NOTE: To ensure proper commenting one should employ better naming of identifiers, and comments provided should be for lines of code that are difficult to understand.
